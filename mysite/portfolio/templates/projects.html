Projects

Web Scraping IMDB Top 1000 Movies

For my first project, I delved into the realm of data collection through web scraping. I developed a web scraper in Python, leveraging the capabilities of Selenium and Fake UserAgent libraries. This tool is intricately designed to extract and parse data from IMDB's top 1000 rated movies. It captures an array of movie details, including ranks, titles, release years, ratings, durations, genres, directors, and star casts.

This project was not just about data extraction but also about overcoming common web scraping obstacles such as bot detection and handling proxies. The data acquired is neatly organized into lists and subsequently transformed into a pandas DataFrame, facilitating effortless analysis and data manipulation.

This venture into web scraping showcases the critical skill of collecting data in the absence of APIs and sets a solid foundation for anyone keen on the art of data gathering and analytics. It epitomizes the intersection of technical skill and creative problem-solving in data science.

Weather Monitor for Watersports Enthusiasts

The Weather Monitor project is an innovative solution designed to cater to the needs of watersports enthusiasts. This sophisticated system aggregates real-time weather data from various weather stations, offering a user-friendly dashboard for easy visualization. The primary aim is to assist watersports aficionados in making informed decisions about when to safely engage in their activities on the water.

One of the key features of the system is its user-friendly dashboard, which displays the data in an intuitive format, accessible via any device connected to the club's network. This allows sailors and club officials to check the current weather conditions at a glance, from anywhere at any time.

The implementation of this weather monitoring system stands as a testament to our commitment to leveraging technology for safety and excellence in sailing sports. It represents a significant step forward in the club's infrastructure, positioning it at the forefront of technological integration in sailing.

Rockfall Risk Evaluation

As part of a team project during my studies at the FHNW for BSc in Data Science, we embarked on a critical assessment of the risk of fatalities on the Cantonal Road B28 due to rockfall. We scrutinized data collected from a road in Schiers, GR, which runs dangerously close to a steep cliff prone to frequent rockfall. The existing safety nets, designed to catch falling rocks, were aged and no longer met safety standards, posing a potential failure within the next year that could lead to severe accidents or even fatalities.

Our objective was to calculate the probability of fatalities until the nets were replaced within a year. By law, the road would have to be closed if the probability of a fatality exceeded 0.0001 per year.

We employed a methodological approach that included explorative data analysis (EDA), data cleaning, visualization, and probability calculations. Our findings, after conducting a Monte Carlo simulation, revealed a fatality probability of 0.000212 per year, which surpasses the legal threshold. Based on our results, we strongly recommended closing the road to prevent loss of life and suggested identifying alternative routes to divert traffic and informing the public about the closure and planned alternatives through an information campaign.

The project not only showcased our ability to handle large datasets and apply statistical models but also emphasized the importance of data science in making informed decisions that can save lives.
